Calcium

Status: Adequate



Risks

Inadequate calcium intake in adults can lead to various health risks and complications, as calcium is an essential mineral with crucial functions in the body.

  • Osteopenia
  • Osteoporosis
  • Cataracts
  • Dental Changes
  • Muscle Problems
  • Extreme Fatigue
  • Severe Problems

    • Inadequate calcium intake over the long-term can contribute to the development of osteoporosis, a condition characterized by weakened and brittle bones. To maintain optimal health, it's crucial for adults to ensure an adequate intake of calcium through a balanced diet, which may include dairy products, leafy green vegetables, nuts, seeds, and fortified foods or supplements if necessary.


    Benefits

    Calcium is essential for adults for:

  • Bone Health, Teeth Health
  • Muscle Function, Blood Clotting
  • Nerve Transmission
  • Blood Pressure Regulation
  • Cell Signaling
  • Hormone Secretion
  • Hypocalcemia Prevention
  • Reduced Risk of Chromic Diseases

    • Recommendations

    However, excessive calcium intake can lead to hypercalcemia and kidney stones.It's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional to determine individual needs and ensure a balanced and healthy diet.
